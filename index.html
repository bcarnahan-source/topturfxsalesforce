Essential Aftercare Tips

Nice work, Mike — your lawn is on its way to greatness.

Your aeration & overseeding service is complete! Ashley R. punched thousands of cores into your soil and laid down premium Fescue seed. Now it's your turn to help those seeds thrive. Here's exactly what to do over the next 4 weeks.

▶ Watch: Lawn Care After Aeration & Seeding | Essential Aftercare Tips

💧 Watering (The #1 Most Important Thing)

Keep the seed moist — not soaked, not dry. Water lightly 2–3 times per day for the first 2 weeks (5–10 minutes per session). The goal is to keep the top ½ inch of soil consistently damp so seeds can germinate. After week 2, transition to deeper, less frequent watering (once daily, 15–20 minutes).

🚫 What NOT to do:

• Don't mow until new grass reaches 3–4 inches (typically 3–4 weeks)
• Don't rake the soil plugs — they'll break down naturally in 1–2 weeks and feed your soil
• Don't apply weed killer for at least 6–8 weeks — it will kill new seedlings
• Don't let heavy foot traffic on seeded areas while germinating

📅 Your 4-Week Timeline

Week 1–2: Water 2–3x daily, short sessions. You'll see germination starting around day 7–10. Leave soil plugs alone.

Week 2–3: New grass appears! Transition to once-daily deeper watering. Still no mowing or heavy traffic.

Week 3–4: Grass reaches 3–4 inches. Time for your first mow — set blade HIGH (remove only top ⅓). Soil plugs should be fully dissolved.

Week 4+: Resume normal lawn care routine. If you see thin spots, use your courtesy seed bag to touch up. Need us to come back? We'll reseed free of charge after your first mow.

🌱 Your courtesy bag of seed was left near your front door. Use it on any thin spots you notice after germination. If you need more coverage, just reply to this email and we'll schedule a free service call.

Results start showing in as little as 4 weeks. By spring, you'll have the thickest lawn on Oak Hill Dr. We'll check in at your 30-day follow-up inspection!
